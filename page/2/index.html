左脑笔记：Thinking Patterns in the East and West

发表于 2020-07-18 分类于 English
Set thinking tendencies （思维定势）

The ancient Greek and Chinese philosophies respectively played a vital role in the formation of different thinking patterns in the east and west.

The differences in traditional thinking patterns can roughly be summarized as the following.

eg:

Traditional Chinese Medicine and Western medicine

TCM regards human body as an organic whole, taking the connections of all parts of the body into consideration.

Western medicine divides human bodies into different parts and treat them accordingly.

Painting

In China, a synthesis of different art forms: calligraphy, painting, poetry and stamps, while in the west, painting is painting and poetry is poetry.

Concrete vs. Abstract

Concrete thinking (thinking in image) refers to the thinking pattern by which people make an understanding of something abstract through analysis, generalization, analogy, association or metaphor.

Tortuous vs. Straight

The problem-solving approaches and thinking patterns of English-speakers from the US were more linear and direct than that of th Semitic Asian, Romance language, or Russian speakers.

Asians employed a circular approach.

eg: Concept of time

To the Chinese people, time is circular, just as the changing of Yin and Yang. With this idea, the Chinese people use time more freely and elastically, they are not so punctual.

Inductive vs. Deductive

Traditionally, Chinese people tended to summarize or make an induction of their experience or insight to form vague theories, concepts or categories on the basis of practice or intuition. Such concepts or categories, like holy words or maxims, without clear definition or careful deduction, seldom became a scientific system.

While in the past Aristotle, the ancient Greek philosopher, stressed deduction.

Subjective vs. Objective

Subjectivity is the characteristic of intuition. Chinese people stress more on subjective thinking. They follow “Oneness Between Man and Nature” and tend to describe something from their own point of view, thus, “somebody” is often used as the subject of a sentence.

左脑笔记：Meaning (Semantics)

发表于 2020-07-18 分类于 English
Definition:

Semantics is the study of meaning in language.

Meaning has been studied for thousands of years by philosophers, logicians and linguists.

eg: What does “capitalist” mean to you?

Seven types of meaning:

  1. conceptual meaning
  2. connotative meaning
  3. social meaning
  4. affective meaning
  5. reflected meaning
  6. collocative meaning
  7. thematic meaning
Conceptual meaning （概念意义/外延意义）

Also called ‘denotative’ or ‘cognitive’ meaning.

Refers to logical, cognitive or denotative.

Concerned with the relationship between a word and the thing it denotes, or refers to.

Connotative meaning （内涵意义）

What is communicated by virtue of what language refers to.

Involving the ‘real world’ experience one associates with expression when one uses or hears it.

Unstable: they vary considerably according to culture, historical period, and the experience of the individual.

Denotation and Connotation

eg: Mr. Smith is a white man. He was looking rather green the other day. He has been feeling blue these days. When I saw him, he was in brown mood, I hope he’ll soon be in the pink again.

Social meaning

Dialect: the language of a geographical region or of a social class.

Time: the language of the 18th.

Status: polite, colloquial, slang, etc.

Affective meaning

Reflecting the personal feeling of the speaker, including his attitude to the listener, or his attitude to something he is talking about.

Such as: 滚出去！

‘Colorful’ meaning:

Commendatory: tough-minded; resolute; shrewd

Derogatory: ruthless; obstinate

Reflected meaning

What is communicated through association with another sense of the same expression. Such as,

When you hear ‘click the mouse twice’, you think of Gerry being hit twice by Tom.

also: make water

Collocative meaning （搭配意义）

The associations a word acquires on account of the meaning of words which tend to occur in its environment.

eg: pretty; handsome

Thematic meaning （主题意义）

What is communicated by the way in which a speaker or writer organizes the massage, in terms of ordering, focus, and emphasis.

The Referential Theory

Referential theory: the theory of meaning which relates the meaning of a word to the thing it refers to, or stand for.

Sense（涵意）: the abstract properties of an entity.

Reference（指称）: the concrete entities having those properties.

Every word has a sense, but not every word has a reference.

Sense Relations
Antonymy
  1. Gradable antonymy

3 characteristics:

They are gradable, the denial of one is not necessarily the assertion of the other. eg: “我们先定一个小目标……”

They are graded against different norms a big car.

One member of the pair (usually the higher grade) server as the cover term. eg: 我们只会问“你多大了？”而不是“你多小了？”

  1. Complementary antonymy

Not A= B （非A即B）

  1. Converse antonymy （反向反义词）

成对出现的反义词

Hyponymy (inclusiveness relation)

A is included in/ a kind of B

A superordinate may be missing.

eg: “Color” is not the superordinate of “green, white,……”

Hyponyms may be missing.

eg: uncle, rice in English in contrast to Chinese 叔叔，伯伯，舅舅，稻，谷，米，饭

Componential Analysis

The modern approach to the study of meaning, using semantic feature or semantic component to analyze the meaning of words.

eg:

boy [HUMAN, YOUNG, MALE]

woman [HUMAN, ADULT, FEMALE]

Two words or expressions which have the same semantic components will be synonymous.

Words which have a contrasting components are antonyms.

Words which have all the semantic components of another are hyponyms.

左脑笔记：From Word to Text (Syntax)

Syntax (grammar)

Syntax refers to the study of the rules governing the way different constituents are combined to form sentences in a language, or the study of the interrelationships between elements in sentence structures.

Syntactic relations

Syntactic relations can be analyzed into three kinds, namely, positional relations, relations of substitutability, and relations of co-occurrence.

Positional Relation

For language to fulfill its communicative function, it must have a way to mark the grammatical roles of the various phrases that can occur in a clause.

Positional relation, or word order, refers to the sequential arrangement of words in a language.

eg: The boy kicked the ball.

NP (subjective) NP(objective)

There are three basic ways to classify languages in the world.

  1. word order
  2. genetic classifications
  3. areal classifications

There are 6 possible types of language:

SVO; VSO; SOV; OVS and VOS.

Relation of Substitutability

The Relation of Substitutability refers to classes or sets of words substitutable for each other grammatically in sentences with the same structure.

Relation of Co-occurrence

It means that words of different sets of clauses may permit.

Relations of co-occurrence partly belong to syntagmatic relations, partly to paradigmatic.

eg: preceded by (NP) followed by

Grammatical Construction and its Constituents
Grammatical Construction

any syntactic string of words ranging from sentences over phrasal structures to certain complex lexemes.

eg: an apple; ate an apple; Mary ate an apple

Immediate Constituents

Constituent is a part of a larger linguistic unit. Several constituents together form a construction.

eg:

the girl (NP)

ate the apple (VP)

The girl ate the apple(S)

Endocentric and Exocentric Constructions

Endocentric construction is one whose distribution is functionally equivalent to that of one or more of its constituents.

Exocentric construction refers to a group of syntactically related words where none of the words is functionally equivalent to the group as a whole.

eg:

The boy smiled. (Neither constituent can substitute for the sentence structure as a whole.)

He hid behind the door. (Neither constituent can function as an adverbial.)

John seemed angry. (After division, the connective construction no longer exists.)

Coordination and Subordination

Endocentric constructions fall into two main types.

Coordination is a common syntactic pattern in English and other languages formed by grouping together two or more categories of the same type with the help of a conjunction such as and, but and or.

Subordination refers to the process or result of linking linguistic units so that they have different syntactic status, one being dependent upon the other, and usually a constituent of the other.

eg:

(My brother) can drink (wine).

Swimming in the lake (is fun).

Syntactic Function

The syntactic function shows the relationship between a linguistic form and other parts of the linguistic pattern in which it is used.

Subject

In English, the subject of a sentence is often said to be the agent, or the doer of the action, which the object is the person or thing acted upon by th agent.

eg: Mary slapped John

but is clearly wrong in:

John was bitten by a dog.

In order to account for the case of subject in passive voice, we have two other terms, *”grammatical subject”* and *”logical subject”*.

Another traditional definition of the subject is “what the sentence is about”

eg: Bill is a very crafty fellow.

Object

Object is also a term hard to define. Since, traditionally, subject can be defined as the doer of the action, object may refer to the “receiver” or “goal” of an action, and it is further classified into Direct Object and Indirect Object.

eg: Mother gave my sister (IO) a doll (DO).

DFT频域特性分析

发表于 2020-04-23 更新于 2020-04-27 分类于 数字信号处理

离散傅里叶变换DFT是一种伪变换，是人为创造用于计算机进行数字信号处理的变换。DFT也是一种时域到频域的变换，能够表征信号的频域特性。本文将从循环卷积定理、频域采样定理和用DFT进行频谱三个方面分析DFT的频域特性。

循环卷积定理CCP

序列的循环位移

要了解DFT的循环卷积定理，首先要认识循环位移的概念：
设$x(n)$为有限长序列，长度为$N$，则$x(n)$的循环位移定义为
$$
y(n)=x((n+m))_N R_N (N)
$$

通俗地讲就是将位移出序列主值区间的数补到序列末尾。

频域循环位移定理

如果：
$$
X(k)=DFT[x(n)],0\leq k\leq N-1
\Y(k)=X((k+l))_N R_N(k)
$$
则：
$$
y(n)=IDTFT[Y(k)]=W^{nl}_Nx(n)
$$
也可以直接表示成：
$$
DFT[W^{nm}_Nx(n)]=X((k+m))_NR_N(k)
$$
可以看出频域循环位移定理实际上就是取DFS频域位移后的主值

证明过程：

$$
y(n)=IDFT[Y(k)]=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}Y(k)W_N^{-kn}\
=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}X((k+l))NW_N^{-kn}\qquad\
\quad=W_N^{ln}\frac{1}{N}\sum{k=0}^{N-1}X((k+l))_NW_N^{-(k+l)n}\
$$

令$m=k+l$,则：
$$
y(n)=W_N^{ln}\frac{1}{N}\sum_{m=l}^{N-1}X((m))NW_N^{-mn}\
\qquad\qquad\quad=W_N^{ln}\frac{1}{N}\sum{m=0}^{N-1}X(m)W_N^{-mn}=W_N^{ln}x(n)
$$

循环卷积定理

DFT同样存在类似FT和ZT中时域相乘频域相卷的概念。由于DFT是DFS在时域和频域上取一个周期的伪变换，故循环卷积定理和时域/频域卷积定理略有不同，但推导思路类似。

循环卷积定理：

若：
$$
y_m(n)=h(n)\bigotimes x(n)\
$$
则：
$$
Y_m(k)=DFT[Y_m(n)]_L=H(k)X(k)\qquad k=0,1,2,…,L-1
$$
其中：
$$
H(k)=DFT[h(n)]_L,X(k)=DFT[x(n)]_L
$$

循环卷积的计算方法

除了教材上利用矩阵求解的方法，也可以将序列放在圆周上求解循环卷积：

设序列$x_1(n)$和$x_2(n)$，$y(n)=x_1(n)\bigotimes x_2(n)\quad n=1,2,3,…$ 。将序列$x_1(n)$按顺时针顺序放在外圆周，序列$x_2(n)$按逆时针顺序放在内圆周，将内圆周按顺时针顺序旋转可以求得$y(n)$。


$$
y(1)=x_1(1)x_2(1)+x_2(4)x_1(2)+x_2(3)x_1(3)+x_2(2)x_1(4)\
y(2)=x_1(1)x_2(2)+x_2(1)x_1(2)+x_2(4)x_1(3)+x_2(3)x_1(4)\
y(3)=x_2(3)x_1(1)+x_2(4)x_1(2)+x_2(1)x_1(3)+x_2(2)x_1(4)\
y(4)=x_2(4)x_1(1)+x_2(3)x_1(2)+x_2(2)x_1(3)+x_2(1)x_1(4)
$$

DFT的频域采样定理

如果序列$x(n)$的长度为$M$，只有当频率采样点数$N\geqslant M$时，才有：
$$
x_N(n)=IDFT[X(k)]=x(n)
$$
即可由频域采样$X(k)$恢复原序列$x(n)$，否则产生时域混叠现象。

通过频率采样得到的频域离散序列$x_N(k)$，再对$x_N(k)$进行IDFT得到的序列$x_N(n)$应是原序列以采样点数$N$为周期进行周期化后的主值序列。
