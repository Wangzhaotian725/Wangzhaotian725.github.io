左脑笔记：What is inter-cultural communication?

发表于 2020-07-16 分类于 English

Easy and Difficult

Examples
White Elephant

A thing that is useless and no longer needed, although it may have cost a lot of money.

“You’d better put on more clothes.”

US people like to be independent.

Two Kinds of Fluency

English fluency and communicate fluency.

Language and Culture

Denotation and Connotation

Denotation（指示意义）: conceptual, explicit or surface meaning of a word/term

Connotation（联想意义）: the socio-cultural or the implicit meaning of a word/term

Six Categories
Words in one language that have equivalents or near equivalent in the other language.

eg: fox; pig

Words in two different language that appear to refer to the same thing on the surface, but may actually refer to quite different things or have different connotations.

eg: west wind; east wind

In China “west wind” may bring deep autumn, cold which makes life withering. The east wind, which is warm, blows from the pacific Ocean. As it brings the colorful spring so it is usually used positively, symbolizing progress and prosperity.

In Britain, the east wind, which is cold, blows from the north of European continent, like the west wind in China.

also: compromise

Passive in Chinese but active in English, especially in business negotiation.

Words in one language that seem to have equivalent in the other, but these so-called equivalents are only partly equivalent.

eg: intellectual; family

also: carder（干部）

In English-speaking countries, many people don’t know what it really means.

also: social science

In Chinese, it covers all the fields except the ones in the natural science and applied science.

Words that have connotations in one language but none in the other.

eg: pine tree; bamboo; plum blossom; orchid; chrysanthemum

also: 13

Words that are represented by fewer words in one language but by more in the other language.

eg: teacher; uncle; aunt; niece; sister; brother

also: 律师; advocate; attorney; barrister; lawyer; solicitor

also: 副; vice-chairman; associate professor; deputy director

Words in one language that find no counterparts in the other language.

eg: Beetles

Metaphor and Association

Metaphors are found in all language.

Animals

a bull in a china shop: an angry, snorting bull charging into a shop filled with exquisite fragile porcelain

owl: English: stands for wisdom, as wise as an owl

​ Chinese: a sign of bad luck

peacock: English: stands for someone who is vain and fond showing off, as vain as a peacock, as proud as a peacock.

also: bear; dragon; Chinese Zodiac（生肖）

Plant Words

similarities:

laurel: symbol for glory

differences:

eg: clover; daffodil

Color Words

in English eg:

white man: 忠实可靠的人

green: 脸色不好

blue: 闷闷不乐

in a brown study: 心事重重

in the pink: 振作

Taboos

eg: Thou shalt not speak my name in vain.
